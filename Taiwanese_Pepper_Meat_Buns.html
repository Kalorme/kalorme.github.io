Taiwanese Pepper Meat Buns

whattocooktoday.com6 large buns45min20min95min

Make this absolutely scrumptious Taiwanese hu jiao bing with a crispy crust and delicious aromatic peppery meat filling.

Buns:

  • 375 g flour
  • 5 g instant yeast or active dry yeast
  • 6 g salt
  • 13 g sugar
  • 215 ml warm water

Lamination dough

  • 75 g flour
  • 50 g softened butter
  • 2 g salt
  • 4 g ground Sichuan peppercorn

Filling:

  • 150 g minced pork
  • 300 g pork neck or another pork cut
  • 4 g ground black pepper
  • 1 g ground white pepper
  • 2 g ground Szechuan peppercorns
  • 2 g five spice powder
  • 4 g salt
  • 12 g sugar
  • 3 g MSG
  • 15 ml Chinese cooking wine
  • 15 ml light soy sauce
  • 7 ml dark soy sauce
  • 8 ml sesame oil
  • 4 g minced ginger
  • 4 stalks green onion finely sliced

Glaze:

  • 30 g honey
  • 15 ml water

Dipping sauce:

  • Gochujang
  • Mayo
  • Soy sauce
  • Honey
  • Lemon juice

Prepare the filling (best to marinate overnight):

  1. Add all the ingredients for the filling, except for green onion and pork neck in a large mixing bowl. Stir to blend everything until a paste is formed. Cut the pork neck into small cubes and add to the mixture. Cover and let chill in the refrigerator for 8-12 hours

Prepare the bun dough:

  1. If you use active dry yeast, dissolve the yeast in some water (from the 170 ml warm water) and add a small pinch of sugar. Stir and let it sit for 10 minutes to activate. It will turn foamy. If you use instant yeast, you can skip this step
  2. Place flour, sugar, salt, and yeast in a mixing bowl. Gradually add in the warm water until it comes into a dough. Continue to knead until the dough is smooth and not sticky. Cover and let rise in a warm place until it's doubled in size, about 1 hour.

Lamination dough:

  1. Combine flour, Sichuan pepper, butter and a pinch of salt in a bowl. Knead with a spatula until a cohesive mass is formed.
  2. Divide into eight equally sized balls of around 15 g each.

Making the buns

  1. Once the bun dough doubled in size and doesn’t spring back when pressed, divide it into eight equal pieces.
  2. Lightly dust the work surface and roll out the bun dough. Flatten a lamination ball in a disk and place in the center of the rolled out bun dough.
  3. Fold the bun dough inwards to surround the lamination disk. Flatten the combined dough. And roll it into a long oval.
  4. Roll the oval up in a cylindrical shape, with a spiral in the centre.
  5. Flatten the cylinder by pressing down on the centre spiral.
  6. A dough ball with a spiral pattern should be formed.

Wrapping:

  1. Remove the filling from the fridge and then add the chopped green onion and stir again to combine. Divide the filling equally into eight portions. You can also choose not to combine the chopped green onion into the meat filling like how it's traditionally done. Preheat oven to 200°C
  2. Flatten one dough ball and roll out into a round circle. Scoop filling and place it on the middle of the dough. Gather the edge of the dough to pinch and seal. Repeat with the rest of the dough and meat filling
  3. If you didn't combine the green onion in the meat mixture earlier, don't seal the bun just yet, scoop some of the chopped green onion and stuff on top of the meat filling, gently push it in and then gather the edge of the dough to pinch and seal. It's a bit harder to wrap this way, but that's how it's traditionally done. Continue with the rest of the dough and meat filling
  4. Place the wrapped buns, seam side down on a baking sheet lined with a parchment paper. Brush the top surface of the buns with honey + water mixture evenly and then gently pick up the buns and dip the bun into the sesame seeds (optional).

Baking:

  1. Place in the preheated oven and bake for the next 20 minutes or until the buns are golden brown and the meat filling is cooked through inside
  2. Remove from the oven and then transfer to a cooling rack. Serve warm
