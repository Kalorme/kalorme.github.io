Meat and Potato Pie

krumpli.co.uk410min3h4h 10min

When it comes to comfort food it is hard to beat a pie, this classic British Meat and Potato Pie takes a while but it is a glorious simple slice of old fashioned cooking.

  • 350 g Stewing Beef
  • 125 g Onion
  • 50 g Carrot
  • 25 g Celery
  • 1 Bay Leaf
  • 50 ml Worcesertshire Sauce
  • 1 Tbsp Oil
  • 100 g Floury Potatoes
  • 150 g Waxy Potatoes
  • Salt to Taste
  • Black Pepper to Taste
  • 300g Flour
  • 75 g Lard
  • 75 g Butter
  • 4-6 Tbsp Water
  • Pinch of Salt
  • 1 Egg
  1. Cut the beef into a 2-3cm dice.
  2. Add the oil to a medium pan over a medium high heat.
  3. Season the beef generously with salt and pepper then fry in two batches to get a nice colour, then set aside.
  4. Slice the onion into half moon shapes and then cut the carrot into 1 cm thick rounds.
  5. Finely dice the celery.
  6. Return the pan to the heat but reduce to medium.
  7. Add the onion, celery and carrot and cook until the onions soften. Around 10 minutes or so.
  8. As the vegetables cook they will release liquid, use this to scrape the bottom of the pan and remove any crispy bits.
  9. Return the beef and any resting juices to the pan.
  10. Add the Worcestershire sauce and reduce by half.
  11. Pour in just enough water to cover the beef and add the bay leaf.
  12. Reduce the heat to low add a lid and allow to simmer for 90 minutes.
  13. Peel and cut the floury potato into a 1cm dice and cut the waxy potatoes into a 2cm dice, do not peel the waxy potatoes.
  14. Remove the lid and add these potatoes to the pan.
  15. Turn up the heat to medium and cook for another 30-35 minutes until the floury potatoes begin to break down and the waxy potatoes are cooked.
  16. Remove this stew from the heat and allow it to cool completely!
  17. Whilst the pie filling is cooling add the flour, lard, butter and salt to a food mixer and pulse to create breadcrumbs. *See Note
  18. Transfer these breadcrumbs to a mixing bowl and add the water a little at a time whilst mixing with the back of a knife.
  19. You want to just bring the pastry together and not knead it.
  20. Wrap in cling film and place in the fridge whilst the filling cools.
  21. When you are ready cut the pastry in half and roll out the first half to 2-3mm thick.
  22. Add this to the base of a 22-25cm pie plate.
  23. Roll out the second piece of pastry to the same thickness, maybe a little thicker.
  24. Add the cooled pie filling to the plate.
  25. Brush the edges of the bottom layer of pastry with beaten egg and add the top sheet of pastry.
  26. Work around the edges of the pie with a fork to seal.
  27. Brush the pie with more beaten egg and add some decoration if you want.
  28. Place in the oven and cook at 200°C or 400°F for 30-35 minutes.
  29. Allow the pie to sit for 10 minutes before cutting and serving.
