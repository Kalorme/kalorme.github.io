Vietnamese Beef Stew

vickypham.com510min2h 20min3h

A traditional Vietnamese beef stew, Bò Kho is hearty, flavorful, and full of Southeast Asian flavors. It’s perfect for a comforting and satisfying meal any day. It is made with tender beef chuck, sometimes beef tendons, aromatic spices, and carrots, all slow-cooked to perfection in a savory tomato-based broth. Enjoy this dish with steamed rice, a toasty baguette, or rice noodles.

  • 1 kg beef boneless beef chuck, ox tails, boneless beef shank, beef tendons
  • 3 large carrots
  • 2 large stalks fresh lemongrass
  • 5 cm knob ginger
  • 5 g red curry powder
  • 5 g sea salt
  • 15 g granulated cane sugar
  • 15 g chicken bouillon powder optional
  • 5 g MSG optional
  • 30 g minced garlic divided
  • 30 g minced shallots divided
  • 30 ml vegetable oil or annatto oil
  • 30 g tomato paste
  • 355 ml coconut soda or juice
  • 950 ml water
  • 1 cinnamon stick cassia bark
  • 3 star anise
  • Cilantro
  • Thai basil
  • Salt
  • Ground black pepper
  • Lemon or lime juice
  1. Prep the ingredients. Cut beef steaks and tendons (if using) into 2.5 cm cubes. If using oxtail, leave them as is. Peel the carrots and cut them into 2.5 cm chunks. Cut the lemongrass into 10 cm segments. We will be using the leafy tops as well. Smash the meaty stalks with a mallet or something heavy to release their aroma and flavor. Tie the lemongrass bunch together with cooking twine or with its own leafy stems. Slice the ginger into thick coins and give each coin a gentle smash.
  2. Add beef cubes to a medium bowl and marinate with red curry powder, salt, sugar, chicken bouillon powder, MSG, garlic (half), and shallots (half) for at least 30 minutes, or overnight in the fridge for better results.
  3. To the bottom of a large pot with a lid, add vegetable or annatto oil and heat on medium-high. Add garlic (the other half), shallots (the other half), lemongrass bundle, ginger slices, and marinated beef cubes. Pan fry until fragrant until you get a nice color and caramelization on the beef (about 2 minutes). Stir in tomato paste.
  4. Add coconut soda and water. Braise with the lid on for two hours on low heat or until meat is tender.
  5. Add carrots, cinnamon stick, and star anise. Continue cooking with a covered lid on a low simmer until carrots are chopstick-tender (about 20 minutes). If the liquid is evaporating off too much, add a bit more water. Discard the lemongrass bundle, ginger slices, cinnamon stick and star anise.
  6. When ready to serve, garnish with cilantro or Thai basil.
  7. To make the dipping sauce, mix together one part salt to ½ part ground black pepper. Squeeze in lemon or lime to taste (one part juice).
  8. Serve with a toasted French baguette, or steamed white rice. If making this with rice noodles, add a bit more hot water to the stew to make it soupier. Season the broth with fish sauce to taste, if needed.
