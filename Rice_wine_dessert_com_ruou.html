Rice wine dessert (com ruou)

en.petitchef.com410 min30 min72 hrs

Vietnamese Desert rice wine (southern style)

Rice

  • 400 g glutinous rice
  • 500 ml water add more or less

Yeast

  • 8 g Vietnamese yeast-ball
  • 15 g salt
  • 240 ml water
  • Banana leaves optional
  1. Rinse 400 g sweet rice about 2 times. Then add in 500 ml water. Cook the sweet rice in a rice cooker.
  2. Once it’s done, open the rice cooker, shuffle the sweet rice, and close the rice cooker again. Let it keep warm for about 10 minutes.
  3. While the sweet rice is keeping warm, mix 240 ml water with 15 g salt. Set it aside.
  4. Smash 8 g dried yeast into a powder. Using a fine strainer, sift the dried yeast to remove any lumps. Set aside.
  5. Place 2 layers of banana leaves in a large colander. After keeping the sweet rice warm for 10 minutes, scoop it onto the banana leaves.
  6. Spread the sweet rice around and let it cool for 7–10 minutes. Then sprinkle the dried yeast over the sweet rice using a strainer for even distribution. Shuffle the sweet rice and dried yeast together.
  7. Cut the banana leaves into long strips about 12 cm long and 2.5 cm wide. Pour some of the saltwater mixture into a small bowl.
  8. Dip your hands in the saltwater mixture, take some of the sweet rice, and roll it into a small ball. Dip the ball in the saltwater mixture.
  9. Wrap the ball with a strip of banana leaf and place it into a glass bowl with a lid. Repeat until all the sweet rice is rolled into balls.
  10. Sprinkle a little bit of saltwater on top and discard the leftover saltwater. Cover with plastic wrap, then place the lid over the bowl.
  11. Put the bowl in the oven for 1–2 days, making sure not to turn on or use the oven during this time.
  12. After 1–2 days, remove the bowl from the oven and take off the lid. There should be a fermented smell and some liquid inside the bowl. Remove the banana leaves.
  13. Store in the fridge until ready to serve.
