Jamaican Beef Baozi’s

Personal42 hrs30 min3 hrs

Fusion between Vietnamese Bánh Bao’s and Jamaican beef patty.

Bao bun yeast mixture:

  • 150 ml lukewarm milk
  • 12.5 g sugar
  • 4 g dry instant yeast

Bao bun dough:

  • 300 g cake flour
  • 60 g corn starch
  • 70 g sugar
  • 4 g baking powder
  • 1 eggwhite
  • 15 ml lemon juice
  • 28 ml oil
  • 1.5 g salt
  • 10 g curry powder
  • 10 g turmeric

Beef patty filling:

  • 80 g roughly chopped scallions
  • 40 g diced white onion
  • 5 g fresh thyme leaves
  • 5 g paprika
  • 5 g garam masala
  • 2 g garlic powder
  • 2 g onion powder
  • 2 g ground allspice pimento berries
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper depends on how hot the pepper is
  • 7 g peeled ginger
  • 10 ml olive oil
  • 600 g ground beef
  • 40 g fresh breadcrumbs
  • 160 ml beef stock
  • 10 ml fish sauce
  • 12 g browning burnt brown sugar
  • 15 g kosher salt
  • 4 g ground black pepper
  • 1 sheet gelatin
  • 2 packets of grated cheese
  1. Make the baozi dough by combining all dry ingredients. Add yeast mixture and knees until a dough is formed. Add oil, mix until integrated and, let rest under damp towel for 2-3 hours.
  2. Make the filling: Combine the scallions, onion, thyme, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, all spice, garlic, peppers, and ginger in the bowl of a food processor and purée until minced.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium. Add the vegetables and cook until soft, 2 minutes. Add the beef and cook until brown, 3 to 5 minutes. Add the breadcrumbs, beef stock, browning, salt, pepper, and gelatin and cook until thick, about 25 minutes.
  4. Make bao wrappers and fill with beef patty filling and a generous amount of shredded cheese.
  5. Bake in oven at 200°C until golden brown and ready
