Cheesy Enchiladas

thesaltymarshmallow.com635 min25 min1 hrs

These Cheese Enchiladas are filled with creamy cheese and topped with a homemade red enchilada sauce-- a perfectly cheesy meal!

Enchilada sauce:

  • 40 g canola oil
  • 40 g corn starch
  • 40 g chili powder
  • 10 g garam masala
  • 950 ml chicken broth
  • 225 g tomato sauce
  • 10 g dried oregano
  • salt to taste

Filling:

  • 6 tortillas
  • 2 packets of Mozzarella
  • 2 packets of shredded cheese
  • 1 packet of bell pepper mix
  • 2 cans of kidney beans
  • 2 cans of corn
  • Sweet potato optional
  • Minced meat (optional)

To Make The Enchilada Sauce

  1. Heat oil in a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. When the oil shimmers and is hot, add corn starch, followed by the chili powder and ground cumin.
  2. Stir the mixture until the cornstarch and spices are combined with the oil. Continue stirring and cooking until mixture is very fragrant-- about 2 minutes.
  3. Pour the chicken broth into the spice mixture. Stir or whisk no lumps remain.
  4. Pour tomato sauce into the pan and stir until smooth, thickened and brought to a boil. Boil for 1 minute. Stir in oregano and season to taste with salt.

To Assemble The Enchiladas

  1. Spoon about 60 ml of the enchilada sauce into a pan and spread over the bottom.
  2. Working one at a time, dip the corn tortillas into the hot enchilada sauce until soft and transfer to a plate. Top each dipped tortilla with about 20 g grated cheese and roll-up.
  3. Transfer each rolled tortilla from the plate into the baking dish. Once baking dish is full, spread remaining enchilada sauce over the top of the enchiladas and sprinkle remaining cheese over the top.
  4. Bake at 180Â°C for 20-25 minutes or until bubbly can cheese is melted.

Serving size 2 enchiladas
