Spicy Peanut Udon

marionskitchen.com415 min15 min30 min

Peanutty, comforting stir-fried udon!

  • 400 g udon noodles
  • 5 spring onions
  • 50 g vegetable oil
  • 8 g ground Sichuan peppercorns
  • 4 g ground black peppercorns
  • 8 g chilli powder
  • 2 whole star anise
  • 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 8 g finely grated ginger
  • 500 g minced meat
  • 250 ml (chicken) stock
  • 60 ml light soy sauce
  • 30 g peanut butter
  • 10 ml white vinegar
  • 10 g cornstarch mixed with 15 ml water
  • 15 g sesame oil
  1. To prepare the noodles, bring a large pot of water to the boil. Add the noodles and let them soak for about a minute. Then use tongs to gently shake the noodles apart. Drain and set aside for later.
  2. To prepare the spring onions, finely chop the pale part and set aside. Then finely sliced the green parts and keep those separate.
  3. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large frying pan over medium heat.
  4. Add the Sichuan peppercorns, chilli powder and star anise. Cook the spices for about a minute or until fragrant.
  5. Add the garlic, pale part of the spring onion and the ginger. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds.
  6. Now increase the heat to high and add in the mince. Stir-fry until the meat is cooked.
  7. Then stir through the stock, soy sauce, peanut butter and vinegar.
  8. Simmer for about 4-5 minutes to allow the flavours to infuse.
  9. Remove and discard the star anise.
  10. Stir through the cornflour mixture. Simmer for a further minute or until the sauce has thickened.
  11. Then toss through the cooked noodles. Remove from heat and toss through the sesame oil.
  12. Divide among serving bowls and top with the green part of the spring onions.
