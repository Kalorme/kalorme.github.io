Ajitama

wayoframen.com2 people3 min7 min72 hrs

Serve in ramen, as a snack, or with your meal.

  • 8 eggs

Marinade

  • 100 ml soy sauce
  • 100 ml mirin
  • 500 ml water
  • 10 g sugar
  • Bonito flakes

Topping

  • 1-2 pinches salt
  • 2-3 dashes shichimi seven-flavor chili pepper
  • 1-2 tsp edible rayu Japanese chili sauce (optional)
  • 2-3 pinches sliced green onions

Soft-boiled Eggs

  1. Use an egg hole puncher or pin to poke a small hole in the base of each egg. (optional)
  2. Add enough water to a pot so eggs will be fully submerged.
  3. Bring water to a boil.
  4. Carefully add eggs to the pot of boiling water so they don't crack.
  5. Set a timer for 6 minutes 30 seconds - 7 minutes (for soft-boiled eggs). Leave pot uncovered.
  6. While eggs are boiling, fill a bowl or container with plenty of ice and cold water.
  7. When timer goes off, carefully dump out boiling water. Use a spoon to immediately submerge eggs into the ice bath.

Marinade for Eggs

  1. While the eggs are in the ice bath, mix the marinade in a jar or sealable container.
  2. Pour soy sauce, mirin, and water into the jar or container.
  3. After eggs have been in the ice bath for 3-4 minutes, carefully remove and peel them.
  4. Add eggs to the marinade.
  5. Cover the eggs and marinade with a paper towel. Ensure that the paper towel absorbs the liquid. This weight will make sure the eggs are fully submerged.
  6. Seal the jar or container with a lid.
  7. Place in the refrigerator for 1-2 days

After Eggs are Marinated

  1. After the eggs have marinated, remove them from the marinade with a spoon.
  2. On a cutting board, wrap each egg with a short piece of fishing line. Wrap the line around the egg one time and pull the line ends until the egg slices open.
  3. Garnish with your favorite topping and serve.
