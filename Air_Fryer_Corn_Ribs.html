Air Fryer Corn Ribs

allrecipes.com15 min

These air fryer corn ribs are easy to make and feature cutting the ears lengthwise so they curl up like ribs in this Mexican elote-inspired recipe.

  • 2 ears fresh corn, husked
  • 42 g olive oil
  • 2 g chili powder
  • 0.5 g garlic powder
  • 0.5 g ground black pepper
  • 60 g mayonnaise
  • 10 g lime juice
  • 5 g Mexican-style hot sauce such as Valentina
  • 10 g crumbled cotija cheese
  • 3 g chopped cilantro
  1. Preheat the air fryer to 200 degrees C.
  2. Place an ear of corn on a cutting board and cut in half lengthwise. Cut each half in half lengthwise. Repeat with remaining ear of corn. You should have 8 "ribs" total.
  3. Whisk together olive oil, chili powder, garlic powder, and black pepper in a small bowl until well combined. Brush onto both sides of each rib and place in the air fryer basket.
  4. Cook in the preheated air fryer for 12 minutes, flipping halfway through the cook time.
  5. Meanwhile, whisk together mayonnaise, lime juice, and hot sauce in a second small bowl until thoroughly combined.
  6. Transfer corn ribs to a serving plate. Drizzle sauce over ribs; top with cotija cheese and cilantro.
